# TallyTails ad creative scripts — 5 hooks for first $1k test

Format: vertical 9:16 (TikTok primary), 1:1 (Meta feed), 16:9 (banner fallback). 15-25 seconds each. Sound-optional (Meta auto-mutes Reels).

Each hook can run on the make-ad pipeline at cheap tier ($0 marginal — edge-tts voiceover + Pexels stock B-roll). Premium tier swap available for the winning hook after pre-flight ($0.20-2 per render).

---

## Hook 1: "The rivalry"

**Target:** cold acquisition, broadest reach, concept-first

**First 3 seconds:** TallyTails wordmark fades in fast. Cat mascot animates in from left, dog mascot from right. They lock eyes. Caption big: "THE CATS ARE WINNING."

**Seconds 4-12:** Quick cuts of cat-themed products (fountain, donut bed, scratch post) on a scale tipping toward the cat side. Voiceover (calm female, not aggressive): "Right now, 56% of TallyTails orders are for cats. Dog owners — what gives?"

**Seconds 13-20:** Dog mascot looking dejected. Caption: "Help your tribe." CTA button: "TallyTails.com — pick a side."

**Why it works:** Loss aversion + tribal identity. Doesn't sell a product, sells a position. Travels muted because the visual narrative + captions carry it.

---

## Hook 2: "Cat watching the scoreboard"

**Target:** TikTok-native, pet-content-feed-friendly, retargeting candidate

**First 3 seconds:** POV close-up of a real cat sitting next to a phone screen showing the TallyTails leaderboard. The cat is watching the screen, ears slightly back. Caption: "When the dogs pull ahead overnight."

**Seconds 4-15:** Slow cat-blink. Cat slowly turns to camera. Caption: "Cats. Regroup." Quick cuts of cat-side product close-ups (fountain in motion, motion-orb rolling, bandana on a cat).

**Seconds 16-22:** TallyTails wordmark + "Pick your side. Tip the score." CTA.

**Why it works:** Pure native pet content. Algorithmic match for #cattok feed. No "ad voice" — just a vibe.

---

## Hook 3: "Trial by tribe"

**Target:** narrative-driven, slightly longer-form, more story per click

**First 3 seconds:** Black screen. White text: "Three weeks ago we launched a store." Beat. "We let the customers decide who wins."

**Seconds 4-10:** Quick montage: cat mascot illustration → dog mascot illustration → live leaderboard counter ticking up → real pet photos buying things → split-screen cats vs dogs.

**Seconds 11-18:** Voiceover: "Right now, cats are leading 56% to 44%. Three weeks left. Dog owners aren't going to let this stand. Cat owners aren't going to give it up." Wordmark.

**Seconds 19-25:** CTA: "TallyTails.com — what side are you on?"

**Why it works:** Sets up a narrative the viewer wants to follow. Triggers curiosity (what happens after 3 weeks?). Best for higher-CTR retargeting once cold ads identify interested users.

---

## Hook 4: "Face-off animation"

**Target:** brand-defining, recyclable across many placements

**First 3 seconds:** Pure illustration — the homepage face-off composition animated. Cat mascot left, dog mascot right, scale between them. Scale tilts left, then right, then settles.

**Seconds 4-12:** Real product close-ups projected onto the scale (cat fountain on cat side, LED dog collar on dog side, etc). Caption: "Cat people and dog people, settling it once and for all."

**Seconds 13-20:** Scoreboard reveal animation. Current numbers fade in. Wordmark. CTA: "TallyTails.com — your purchase picks a side."

**Why it works:** Brand-coded ad. Works as static frame too (extract any moment for image placements). Lower CTR than direct hooks but higher brand recall for retargeting.

---

## Hook 5: "Cameo tease"

**Target:** novelty + curiosity, FOMO trigger for return visits

**First 3 seconds:** Cat and dog mascots in their idle poses, looking serious. Caption: "Next week, the capybaras enter the race."

**Seconds 4-12:** Quick cuts of capybara photos (calm, internet-beloved animals). Cat and dog mascots both look concerned. Caption: "Cats and dogs may both lose fans."

**Seconds 13-20:** TallyTails wordmark. Caption: "Capybara Week starts Monday. TallyTails.com — be there."

**Why it works:** Creates FOMO + curiosity. Tests whether the cameo-week mechanic resonates. Press-friendly (Mashable / The Dodo loves these stories). Run as a content drop rather than direct-conversion.

## Production tier choice per hook

| Hook | Cheap tier OK? | Recommend premium tier? |
|---|---|---|
| 1: Rivalry | Yes — captions + stock B-roll work | Maybe for winning version |
| 2: Cat watching | Needs real cat footage — license from Pexels | Yes if it wins organic test |
| 3: Trial by tribe | Yes — narrative-driven, voiceover-led | Premium voice (Cartesia) for warmth |
| 4: Face-off | Yes — illustration-only, animation in Remotion | Veo for product close-up b-roll |
| 5: Cameo tease | Yes — capybara stock is plentiful | Cheap is fine, this is a content drop |

**Initial production cost (cheap tier all 5):** ~$0 marginal (uses existing pipeline)

**If hook 1 or 2 wins the organic pre-flight (Wave 6):** $50-200 to re-render in premium tier with better voice + Veo 3.1 hero clip + Suno music. Worth it for the winning creative.

## Pre-flight signal interpretation

After Wave 6 (5-7 day organic TikTok test on @tallytailsco):

| Engagement (likes + saves + shares per view) | Verdict |
|---|---|
| <0.3% | Hook is broken — re-script or kill |
| 0.3-0.8% | Mediocre — usable as supporting creative, not primary |
| 0.8-1.5% | Solid — fund with paid spend |
| 1.5-3% | Strong — fund hard, this is the hook |
| 3%+ | Viral signal — push paid hard and consider organic content strategy |

If 2+ hooks hit 0.8%+, fund both in the $1k test. If only 1 hits, fund just that one with concentration. If none hit, the concept needs a re-think before spending.

## Tracking per-hook attribution

Each ad creative gets a unique UTM source so we can measure per-hook conversion in the dashboard:

```
tallytails.com/?utm_source=tiktok&utm_medium=paid&utm_campaign=validation_v1&utm_content=hook_1_rivalry
```

Hook conversion metrics tracked separately:
- CPC per hook
- CTR per hook
- Add-to-cart rate per hook
- Purchase rate per hook
- ROAS per hook

Winning hook = scales. Losing hooks = killed. Reallocate budget weekly.
